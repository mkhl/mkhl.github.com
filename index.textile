h2. About

p. Martin Kühl is a software developer from Germany with a special interest in computer languages and development tools and a recent graduate from the "University of Bremen":uni-bremen with a degree in computer science, where he focused on formal methods of software development. He is now looking for an opportunity to change the world.

[uni-bremen]http://www.informatik.uni-bremen.de/

p. Martin enjoys using both Linux and Mac OS X, learning about and applying interesting technology, and searching for the "Quality Without a Name":qwan. His favourite monospaced fonts are "Terminus":terminus and "Inconsolata":inconsolata, and his currently preferred tools are either "TextMate":textmate or "GNU Emacs":emacs for writing code, "Git":git for managing versioning, and "Bash":bash as his shell.

[qwan]http://www.munnecke.com/islands/qwan.htm
[terminus]http://www.is-vn.bg/hamster/
[inconsolata]http://levien.com/type/myfonts/inconsolata.html
[textmate]http://macromates.com/
[emacs]http://www.gnu.org/software/emacs/
[aquamacs]http://aquamacs.org/
[git]http://git-scm.org/
[zsh]http://www.zsh.org/
[bash]http://www.gnu.org/software/bash/

p. He believes that "less is more":less-is-more, that "worse is better":worse-is-better, that "there is no silver bullet":silver-bullet, and that code "must speak for itself":humane-programming.
